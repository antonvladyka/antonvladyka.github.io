Various microscopy techniques.

During my PhD research I mostly used SEM for imaging my nanoscale devices and also for e-beam lithography. In the time of postdoctoral job I worked a bit with gold nanoclusters (as a follow-up of the paper), and to evaluate the size of these clusters I used TEM.

TEM image of Au nanoclusters with ~2nm in diameter.

In our lab we also used few AFM ans STMs, including EC-STM one. STM is mostly used to study single molecular junctions as well as to inverstigate tunneling spectroscopy of different objects such as nucleotides.

Atomically-flat terraces on freshly annealed gold, STM view

Atomically-flat terraces on freshly annealed gold, AFM view

Using AFM, we observed different folding of plasmid single stranded DNA depending of the aggregation objects (such as Au, Ni, Ag nanorods). Tiny result was published.
